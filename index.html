What is Artificial Intelligence?



According to Wikipedia, Artificial intelligence (AI) is intelligence demonstrated by machines, as opposed to the natural intelligence displayed by animals including humans. AI research has been defined as the field of study of intelligent agents, which refers to any system that perceives its environment and takes actions that maximize its chance of achieving its goals.

The term "artificial intelligence" had previously been used to describe machines that mimic and display "human" cognitive skills that are associated with the human mind, such as "learning" and "problem-solving". This definition has since been rejected by major AI researchers who now describe AI in terms of rationality and acting rationally, which does not limit how intelligence can be articulated.

AI broadly refers to applications of technology that perform tasks resembling human cognitive function where machines are capable to imitate intelligent human behavior. AI is a wide-ranging branch of computer science concerned with building smart machines capable of performing tasks that typically require human intelligence. It is the simulation or approximation of human intelligence processes by machines especially computer systems. The machines are programmed to think like humans and mimic their actions and behaviors.


Applications of Artificial Intelligence Technology


The concept of artificial intelligence (AI) arrived in 1950s, which a computer scientist John McCarthy coined the term Artificial Intelligence. AI have been gaining popularity since then as technology advances rapidly, and it continues its function in present generation and even the future. Almost every area of the government, business and professional sectors in our developing society have been integrating artificial intelligence technology.

Artificial Intelligence application is applied into the fields like:

  • Business
  • Education
  • Healthcare
  • Banking
  • Finance
  • Agriculture
  • Gaming
  • Autonomous Vehicles
  • Robotics
  • Other Applications



Photo by Tara Winstead on Pexels

AI applications include advanced web search engines (e.g., Google), recommendation systems (used by YouTube, Amazon and Netflix), understanding human speech (such as Siri and Alexa), self-driving cars (e.g., Tesla), automated decision-making and competing at the highest level in strategic game systems (such as chess and Go). As machines become increasingly capable, tasks considered to require "intelligence" are often removed from the definition of AI, a phenomenon known as the AI effect. For instance, optical character recognition is frequently excluded from things considered to be AI, having become a routine technology.

Artificial intelligence was founded as an academic discipline in 1956, and in the years since has experienced several waves of optimism, followed by disappointment and the loss of funding (known as an "AI winter"), followed by new approaches, success and renewed funding. AI research has tried and discarded many different approaches since its founding, including simulating the brain, modeling human problem solving, formal logic, large databases of knowledge and imitating animal behavior. In the first decades of the 21st century, highly mathematical-statistical machine learning has dominated the field, and this technique has proved highly successful, helping to solve many challenging problems throughout industry and academia.

The various sub-fields of AI research are centered around particular goals and the use of particular tools. The traditional goals of AI research include reasoning, knowledge representation, planning, learning, natural language processing, perception, and the ability to move and manipulate objects.[c] General intelligence (the ability to solve an arbitrary problem) is among the field's long-term goals. To solve these problems, AI researchers have adapted and integrated a wide range of problem-solving techniques—including search and mathematical optimization, formal logic, artificial neural networks, and methods based on statistics, probability and economics. AI also draws upon computer science, psychology, linguistics, philosophy, and many other fields.

Advantages and Disadvantages of Artificial Intelligence Technology


Photo by Pavel Danilyuk on Pexels


With all the hypes and popularity of AI, there have been always a debate about the pros and cons that it contribute. Artificial Intelligence is one of the emerging technologies which tries to simulate human reasoning in AI systems. The advantages of Artificial intelligence applications are enormous and can revolutionize any professional sector.

With the simulation of human intelligence, processes by machines that are especially computer systems include learning the acquisition of information and rules for using it. This is indeed a great challenge for human intelligence to apply into machines.

While the impact of advances of the AI technology is undeniably beneficial, let's discuss some of the major advantages and disadvantages of artificial Intelligence that we could have.


Advantages


  • Automation

    • Automation is one of the important advantages of AI. It describes a wide range of technologies that reduce human intervention in processes. Human intervention is reduced by predetermining decision criteria, subprocess relationships, and related actions, and embodying those predeterminations in machines.

  • Advancement in Medical Applications

    • One of the sophisticated advancement of Artificial Intelligence that have caused a major impact in the modern society is the medical or healthcare systems and applications. Intelligent machines are really of great importance in research, diagnostic and treatment of diseases. These advance technologies allow healthcare professionals to treat and save people they never could have treated and saved before.

  • Human-Error Reduction

    • Since Artificial Intelligence is programmed in machines or embedded in systems following some sets of algorithms, error could be avoidable. Human has the tendency to forget, neglect or ignore some task. Machines on the other hand could increase accuracy and precision.

  • Availability

    • Artificial Intelligence system is available 24/7, no excuses, unless there is an absence of some necessary resources like an electricity or an internet connection for a longer period of time.

  • Faster Decision Making

    • Artificial Intelligence has always been used for making smarter business decisions. It can coordinate data delivery, analyze trends, develop data consistency, provide forecasts, and quantify uncertainties to make the best decisions for the company.

  • Accuracy and Precision

    • Since Artificial Intelligence is programmed using an algorithm, it is accurate and fast. These processes are used specially in big production companies that produce tons of products in a short period of time.

  • Repetitive Jobs Made Easy

    • Human can get tired and exhausted. Machines do not. Artificial Intelligence can carry on repetitive jobs gracefully with no complains.

Disadvantages


  • Production High Costs

    • Machines and tools with Artificial Intelligence are very costly in production, thus requires high budget to acquire those machines or systems.

  • Turns Human Lazy

    • The reliance to Artificial Intelligence will eventually turn the human lazy. Machines and tools can operate with very less or even no human intervention needed.

  • Unemployment Increase

    • This is one of the major drawbacks of Artificial Intelligence. Because most businesses especially those in the big production companies are acquiring those intelligent machines for their production resources, thus leaving many people unemployed. This is highly detrimental in our society since increase of unemployment causes social problems.

  • Ethical Issues

    • Machines and tools with Artificial Inteligence is as much as intelligent as humans but they do not possess high intellect as humans. Thus the use of artificial Intelligence causes an ethical issues in some areas among various sectors, especially in Healthcare.

  • No Initiatives

    • Machines simply designed to act according to the program they are capable of doing. They cannot do the things needed to do in case of any untoward situations. Unlike humans, who can perform and think out of the box, can provide logical and ethical solutions according to what circumstances may ask.
